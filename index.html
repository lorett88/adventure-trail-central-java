Before you go

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a guide?
Each trail card says so honestly: not needed, recommended, or required. Short sunrise walks and boardwalks are fine solo. River caves and the Mount Slamet summit require certified local guides — that's safety and local regulation, not a tourist tax. Guide fees on this site are the fair local rates.
When is the best season?
Dry season: roughly April–October, with June–September most reliable. Caves are strictly dry-season only — flash floods in river caves are lethal. Some mountains close for trail recovery early in the year (Prau, typically Jan–Mar). Each card lists its own best months.
How fit do I need to be?
Most trails here are Easy or Moderate — if you can climb stairs for 30–45 minutes, Sikunir, the waterfalls and the coast walks are yours. Prau needs decent legs. Slamet and the full cave expedition need real fitness and experience. The difficulty tag on each card is honest, not marketing.
Where should I base myself?
Three bases cover everything: Purwokerto for Baturraden's waterfalls and Slamet, Dieng village for the sunrise trails and craters, and Gombong/Kebumen for the caves and coast. Each trail card names its base town. Trains connect all three to Jakarta, Bandung and Yogyakarta.
Are the prices accurate?
They're local rates reviewed July 2026 — entrance fees at small sites drift by a few thousand rupiah each year, guide rates by season and group size. Treat them as solid estimates, and check our companion Central Java Fair Price Guide for everything else you'll pay on the trip.
